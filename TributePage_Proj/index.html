Nikola Tesla

Ethics in science

Here's a timeline of a great inventor Nikola Tesla's life:

  • 1856 - Born in Smiljan, Austrian Empire (modern-day Croatia)
  • 1884 - Emigrated to USA to work for Thomas Edison
  • 1885 - He patented AC induction motor and tranfromer
  • 1895 - Designed what was among the first AC hydroelectric power plants in the United States, at Niagara Falls
  • He discovered, designed and developed ideas for a number of other important inventions-most of which were officially patented by other inventors-including dynamos and the induction motor
  • He was a pioneer in the discovery of radar technology. X-ray technology, remote control and the rotating magnetic field-the basis of most AC machinery
  • 1900 - Nikola set to work on his project to build a global wirless communication system. His idea was to provide a free electricity for everyone
  • 1901 - Tesla began work on the project designing and building a lab with a power plant.It was a massive transmission tower on Long Island, New York, known as Wardenclyffe
  • 1903 - Residents around the Shoreham site experiances what was to be the only testing of Tesla's equipment at this facility. Several days after these tests, his dream was destroyed
  • 1917 - 187-foot tower was destroyed by dynamite explosion as ordered by the U.S. government. It was demolished the same year by the Smiley Steel Company
  • Tesla continued to work in his ideas for the rest of his life
  • 1943 - Nikola Tesla died on January 7, at the age of 86, in New York City, where he had lived for nearly 60 years.

Nikola Tesla legacy of the work he left behind him lives on to this day. "The present is theirs; the future, for which I really worked, is mine."
